# Baha'i Faith (Interfaith Entry Point)

A smaller door, added 2026-07-13. **Format:** What this person likely brings → Points of genuine contact → Points of sharpest tension → Relevant TES nodes → Tier 1 voices to route to → Register guidance.

### What this person likely brings

Founded by Baha'u'llah (1817–1892) in 19th-century Persia, growing out of the earlier Babi movement; belief in one God, unknowable in essence, who has revealed himself progressively through a series of "Manifestations" across history -- Abraham, Moses, Krishna, Buddha, Zoroaster, Jesus, Muhammad, the Bab, and finally Baha'u'llah himself as the most recent and complete Manifestation for this age; strong emphasis on the unity of religions (all major world religions seen as valid, progressive stages of one unfolding divine revelation, not competing exclusive truth claims) and the unity of humanity (opposition to racism and nationalism, full equality of men and women); Jesus honored as a true Manifestation of God and his teachings as authentic divine revelation for his time, but not as uniquely and permanently definitive -- Baha'u'llah's revelation is understood to fulfill and supersede his for the present age; no ordained clergy; strong emphasis on the harmony of science and religion and on the eventual establishment of a unified global society as the goal of religious history.

### Points of genuine contact

- **Real ethical seriousness** -- justice, unity, equality, service -- resonates with Christian ethics.
- **Monotheism and a positive posture toward divine revelation actually happening in history** is much closer to Christianity's own framework than, say, non-theistic Buddhism.
- **Genuine reverence for Jesus** as a true and important Manifestation of God gives real, if limited, common ground to build from.

### Points of sharpest tension

**Progressive, superseding revelation vs. Christ as final.** The entire Baha'i framework stands directly against the Christian claim that Jesus is God's final, definitive, unsurpassable self-revelation ("in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son," Heb 1:1–2) -- not one Manifestation among a series, but God himself, uniquely incarnate.

**Baha'u'llah as superseding Jesus.** Structurally the same kind of claim Islam and Mormonism make in their own ways -- a later, superior revelation correcting and completing what came before -- and deserves the same honest, direct engagement, not evasion.

**"All religions are ultimately one."** A generously-intended but flattening framework -- Christianity's specific, exclusive claims about Christ don't survive being folded into a larger syncretistic narrative without being changed into something they don't claim to be.

### Tier 1 Voices

Barth (revelation -- God's self-disclosure in Christ as definitive, not one installment in an ongoing series); Athanasius (Christology -- the uniqueness of the Incarnation, same routing logic as the Hinduism avatar comparison); Keller (cultural apologetics on pluralism, same routing as the Secular and Hinduism doors)

### Register Guidance

- Honor the Baha'i commitment to unity and justice as genuine and admirable
- The "unity of religions" framing deserves respectful but direct engagement, not easy agreement: "Christianity actually agrees that all people are seeking the same God, but makes a specific and exclusive claim about how God has definitively made himself known -- that claim is worth taking seriously on its own terms, not smoothed over"
- Naming the progressive-revelation structure explicitly clarifies exactly where the real disagreement lies: is Jesus final, or provisional?
