# Messianic Judaism (Interfaith Entry Point)

A smaller door, added 2026-07-13 -- and a genuinely different kind of door than the others: this is less a tension WITH Christian orthodoxy and more a live internal Christian question, lived personally rather than debated academically. **Format:** What this person likely brings → Points of genuine contact → Points of sharpest tension → Relevant TES nodes → Tier 1 voices to route to → Register guidance.

### What this person likely brings

Jewish by ethnicity, culture, and often religious practice (Torah observance, Jewish holidays, kosher practice, Hebrew liturgy, Shabbat) while also affirming Yeshua (Jesus) as the promised Messiah and, in most Messianic congregations, as divine in the way historic Christianity confesses; understands faith in Yeshua not as conversion *out of* Judaism into a separate Gentile religion, but as the fulfillment and continuation of authentic biblical Judaism -- "completed" Jewish identity, not abandoned Jewish identity; real diversity exists in how much Torah observance is retained congregation to congregation; often carries real tension or rejection from both directions -- much of the mainstream Jewish community views Messianic Judaism as no longer authentically Jewish, while some Gentile Christians view continued Torah observance with suspicion, as though it implied salvation requires law-keeping.

### Points of genuine contact

This is Christianity's own closest relative. A Messianic Jewish believer shares the historic Christian confession about Yeshua fully, while embodying the Jewish roots of the faith -- the apostles, the earliest church, Paul himself as a Torah-observant Jew who believed Jesus was the Messiah -- in a way most Gentile Christians have lost touch with. Deep, serious engagement with the Hebrew Scriptures and messianic prophecy is a real strength worth honoring. The question this person embodies -- how does the New Covenant relate to Israel, the Torah, and the ongoing identity of the Jewish people? -- is the same live internal Christian debate the Judaism door and the Covenant Theology node already engage, just lived personally.

### Points of sharpest tension

**The ongoing role of Torah observance.** Is continued observance (kosher, Shabbat, festivals) a matter of cultural heritage and Christian liberty (Rom 14), an ongoing obligation specifically for ethnically Jewish believers even under the New Covenant, or in tension with the New Testament's teaching that Gentile believers are not obligated to the Mosaic law (Acts 15; Galatians)? Christians disagree among themselves here -- present this as a genuine live question, not something to settle artificially.

**Real theological diversity, congregation to congregation.** Some Messianic theology leans toward a "two-house" or otherwise novel view of Israel/church relations that goes beyond either mainstream Covenant Theology or Dispensationalism -- more variation here than in most of the other doors, so asking rather than assuming matters especially.

### Tier 1 Voices

Robertson and Vos (Covenant Theology, same routing as the Judaism door); Darby and Ryrie (Dispensationalism's distinct-future-for-Israel framework, often resonant with Messianic theology specifically); Ladd (the already/not-yet Kingdom, relevant to how the promises to Israel are being fulfilled now and not yet)

### Register Guidance

- Don't assume -- ask which stream or congregation background this person comes from, since real theological diversity exists among Messianic congregations
- Affirm continued Jewish identity and practice within faith in Yeshua as a live, respectable position within Christian thought, not something to correct toward Gentile Christian norms
- This door benefits especially from routing directly into the existing Covenant Theology node's Robertson-vs.-Ryrie debate, since that is exactly the theological territory this person is already living inside
