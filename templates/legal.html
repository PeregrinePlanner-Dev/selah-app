Last updated: July 5, 2026  ·  Draft version

These terms cover both the free Selah — Exploring Theology tool ("Selah," "the Service") at selahexploringtheology.com, and the Selah for Ministry landing page at ministry.selahexploringtheology.com. Selah for Ministry does not yet have accounts, subscriptions, or payment — when those launch, this page will be updated to describe what's collected before those features go live, and the revised version will be posted here with a new date.

1. What Selah Is

Selah is a conversational tool for exploring Christian theology. It draws on more than sixty historic and contemporary Christian voices, Scripture, and a set of guided topics ("nodes") to help you think through questions about faith. It is written from an evangelical Christian frame, not a neutral or interfaith-adjudicating one, and says so plainly when relevant.

Selah is powered by AI (Claude, made by Anthropic) generating responses based on curated theological content. It is not infallible, does not replace study of primary sources, and does not replace guidance from a pastor, counselor, or trusted community.

2. Not Professional Advice, Not a Crisis Service

Selah is not a substitute for professional medical, legal, psychological, or psychiatric care, and it is not a crisis service. If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, or is a minor disclosing an unsafe situation, please contact emergency services, a crisis line, or a trusted adult directly — do not rely on Selah as the sole source of help in an urgent situation.

3. Age & Eligibility

The free Selah tool is open to anyone, at any age, without an account — that's a deliberate design choice, not an oversight. Because it's anonymous, there's no age verification on the free tool itself.

Any future account, subscription, or payment through Selah for Ministry requires the account holder to be 18 or older. Where Selah for Ministry is used with a congregation or group that includes minors, access should be provided through an adult leader's account rather than direct signup by a minor.

4. Privacy — What We Collect

The free Selah tool

  • No account, no login. Nothing identifies you personally to use it.
  • Conversations are held only in server memory during your session. They are not written to a permanent database and are cleared whenever the server restarts. Your session identifier is a random value generated fresh in your browser each time you load the page — it is not a cookie and is not stored on your device.
  • You can download your own conversation as a text file at any time, and optionally upload it in a future visit to continue where you left off. That file lives on your own device — we don't keep a copy.
  • Automated abuse protection. To keep the service available to everyone, we temporarily process the IP address a request comes from to apply rate limits against automated abuse. This is not linked to any personal identity, is not used for any other purpose, and is not kept beyond a short rolling window (the current minute and day).
  • Analytics. We use Google Analytics to understand aggregate traffic (e.g., how many people visit, roughly where from) — this does not include your conversation content.

Selah for Ministry landing page

  • The "Interested?" contact option opens your own email client (a standard mailto: link). Anything you choose to send us that way goes to our inbox directly and is used only to respond to you — nothing is collected by the page itself.

Third-party links

The donation link (Ko-fi) and feedback form (Tally) are operated by those companies, not by us. If you choose to use them, your interaction with them is covered by their own privacy practices, not this page.
