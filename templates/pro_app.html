How to Use Selah for Ministry

The Idea

Selah is a Hebrew word meaning pause — a signal to stop, sit with what you've just heard, and let it sink in. This tool is built on that same rhythm: not to give you all the answers at once, but to take you one layer deeper at a time.

Your Sessions Are Saved Automatically

Every conversation here is saved to your account as you go — no downloading or uploading files. The Your Sessions panel on the left lists your past conversations; click one to pick up right where you left off.

The Layout

The left panel holds your Session Anchor — a running summary of what you've covered — and Your Sessions. The center is the conversation. The right panel shows Scripture passages and theologian arguments as they surface, so you can revisit them without scrolling back. Each Scripture citation includes a Compare Translations link — a quick way to see whether a different English translation would change what the argument actually rests on. Use Switch Topic to move into a different area of theology.
